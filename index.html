MTU Engineering Challenge Series

The MTU Engineering Challenge Series invites students to put their creativity and technical skills to the test through hands-on competitions each semester. This fall features a Roomba time-trial maze, while the spring challenge features an AI poker bot battle!

  • Learn more

Roomba Time Trial (Fall)

Students work in teams to program a Roomba robot vacuum to autonomously navigate a custom maze—the fastest clean run to the finish wins.

Workshop: September 30 · Competition: October 28

  • Learn more

AI Poker Bot (Spring)

Build an AI agent that bluffs, bets, and learns—then face off in tournament play to crown the smartest bot at MTU.

What you’ll do

Learn by building: short workshops, open lab time, and friendly competitions that sharpen your coding, controls, sensing, and AI skills.

Hands-on Engineering

Program real robots and systems—no simulated demos required.

Autonomy & Control

Design navigation strategies, tune sensors, and beat the clock.

Applied AI

Train and test agents against opponents in tournament settings.

Teamwork

Form interdisciplinary teams and iterate fast from idea to demo.
