Roomba Time Trial (Fall)

The Roomba Time Trial Challenge tasks student teams with programming a Roomba robot vacuum to autonomously navigate a custom-built maze. Teams must design and implement navigation strategies that balance speed, accuracy, and adaptability as the robot encounters twists, turns, and dead ends. Each team’s Roomba races the clock—the fastest successful run to the finish wins.

Key Dates

  • Workshop: September 30 — Learn how to program and control the Roomba, review sensors, and test basic navigation.
  • Competition: October 28 — Put your code to the test in the maze time trials.

How It Works

  1. Form a team (or sign up solo and we’ll match you).
  2. Attend the Sept 30 workshop for setup, APIs, and starter code.
  3. Build your strategy (wall-following, waypointing, PID tuning, etc.) and iterate.
  4. On Oct 28, run timed attempts on the official maze layout.

Rules & Scoring (quick version)

  • Autonomy required: no manual driving during runs.
  • Clean finish: the bot must reach the goal without outside assistance.
  • Timing: best valid time across allowed attempts counts.
  • Fair play: no interfering with other teams or the maze.

Full rules and starter code are shared at the workshop and via the sign-up confirmation.

What to Bring

  • Laptop with programming environment (we’ll share requirements).
  • Optional: extra sensors/attachments if permitted by the rules.
